N35 General Quantum Information II

  • Semiclassical Approach to Self-Consistent Classical-Quantum Coupling

    • A self-consistent coupling of classical and quantum subsystems that correctly predicts backreaction effects
    • Decoherence is not a part of the fundamental description, but arises from incomplete access to information
    • key missing piece is the semiclassical phase factor

  • Quantum simulation of excited states with the transcorrrelated Hamiltonian: higher accuracy with fewer qubits

    • add electron correlation terms to the Hatree-Fock Hamiltonian

  • Quantum phase transitions in entanglement complexity

    • disentangling algorithm
    • two quantum phases: disordered and ordered
    • show an order-disorder quantum phase transition with universal critical indexes

  • Analytical framework for describing non-trivial quantum dissipation

    • derivation of quantum master equation in the presence of strong quantum correlations.

  • Quantum Steganography using Coherent states in an Optical Channel

    • Quantum Steganography: a class of methods for covert quantum communication which take advantage of an information gap between the eavesdropper and the communicating parties to send information secretly.
    • encoding messages in coherent state mixtures that would appear as a thermal background to eavesdroppers

  • Quantum state driving: measurements versus pulses

    • means to maintain a system in a certain subspace:

      • quantum Zeno effect: fixing a system to an eigenstate by frequent measurements.
      • applying frequent unitary pulses: induces a Zeno subspace that can also pin the system to an eigenspace.

    • consider making the measurements/pulses dynamical

  • PT-symmetric holographic principle: applying non-Hermitian quantum evolution to quantum simulation

    • Non-Hermitian qubit as platform for efficient quantum simulation
    • revisit this fundamental phenomenon in the simple example of a spinless relativistic quantum particle traveling in (1+1)-dimensional space-time and establish its relation to a spin-1/2 system evolving under a PT-symmetric Hamiltonian.
    • This relation allows us to simulate a 1D scattering problem with a single qubit.
    • summarize and generalize this type of relation as the eigenenergy problem of a bulk system with N spatial dimensions being mapped onto the time evolution of the edge state with (N-1) spatial dimensions governed by a non-Hermitian Hamiltonian.

  • Predicting many properties of quantum systems with chaotic dynamics

    • classical shadow tomography: an universal tomography scheme using randomized measurements
    • entanglement evolution under chaotic quantum dynamics

  • Stochastic Semi-Classical Models of Operator Front Dynamics

    • demonstrate the emergence of distinct universality classes of operator-front hydrodynamics, which leads to complementary insight into the dynamics of quantum many-body systems.

  • Role of Coherence and Degeneracies in Quantum Synchronisation

    • Theorem: In an open quantum system whose dynamics is described by a Markovian master equation with a non-degenerate energy spacings, energy conserving interactions cannot generate phase synchronisation.

  • Exact mean root fidelity and mean-square Bures distance between random density matrices

    • Among various distance measures, Bures distance stands out as an excellent candidate for distinguishability measures between quantum states
    • derived exact analytical results for the mean root fidelity and the mean-square Bures distance using the random matrix theory (RMT) techniques for the pairs of a fixed density matrix and a random density matrix, and two random density matrices, taken from the Hilbert-Schmidt probability measure.

  • Rewinding time with photons

    • https://arxiv.org/pdf/2205.01122.pdf

 

M35 General Quantum Information I

  • Hierarchical graph states of k-uniform and absolutely maximally entangled states

    • characterize the entanglement properties of multipartite quantum states such as graph states, k-uniform, and Absolutely-Maximally-Entangled (AME) states
    • By merging these concepts we provide a remarkable relationship between geometry of graph states and entanglement of the corresponding states.
    • We present a hierarchical procedure that leads us to study the geometric structure of k-uniform and AME states in the graph states representation.

  • Quantum Information Processing with Finite-State Information Transducers

  • Quantum non-Markovianity and information back-flow in quantum teleportation

    • Quantum non-Markovianity (QNM) is a phenomenon associated to the information back-flow from the environment to the dynamical system, i.e., dynamical systems evolving through quantum channels with memory effects.
    • Time-homogeneity is a sufficient condition for non-Markovian dynamics to originate from an information back-flow effect
    • Quantum information is conserved in closed quantum systems that evolve unitarily. https://arxiv.org/pdf/quant-ph/9705003.pdf

  • Memory Structure in Measured Stochastic Processes of Quantum States

Y35 Thermodynamics in Quantum Information I

  • Driven-dissipative dynamics in superconducting circuit lattices coupled to quantum baths

  • The thermodynamic cost of quantum measurements in the circuit quantum electrodynamics architecture

    • energy cost of quantum measurement bounded by kBTI, readout + reset

  • Operator Growth and Symmetry-Resolved Coefficient Entropy in Quantum Maps

    • An explicit way to quantify the complexity of an operator is the Shannon entropy of its operator coefficients over a chosen set of operator basis, dubbed "coefficient entropy". However, it remains unclear if the basis-dependency of the coefficient entropy may result in a false diagnosis of operator growth, or the lack thereof. Here, we will examine the validity of coefficient entropy in the presence of hidden symmetries.
    • Using the quantum cat map as an example, we show that under a generic choice of operator basis, the coefficient entropy fails to capture the suppression of operator growth caused by the symmetries. We further propose "symmetry-resolved coefficient entropy" as the proper diagnosis of operator complexity
